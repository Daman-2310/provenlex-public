## Ecosystem entry points (highest-leverage first)

1. **ALFI (Association of the Luxembourg Fund Industry)** — Annual conference in September. Innovation Lab applications open Q1. Best single access point to fund administrator decision-makers and their VC relationships.

2. **LHoFT Innovation Hub** — Luxembourg fintech accelerator directly backed by government and major banks (BCEE, BGL BNP Paribas, ING). Apply for the cohort; the network alone justifies it.

3. **Luxinnovation Digital Tech Fund** — Non-dilutive grant for digital innovation. Apply in parallel with equity raise. Use for the TIBER-EU audit budget.

4. **Money 20/20 Europe** — Amsterdam, June. Earlybird, Speedinvest, and CommerzVentures all attend. Confirm a speaking or demo slot via the startup application.

5. **SuperVentures Berlin** — B2B SaaS-focused. Mangrove and Notion Capital both send partners. Good second conference after ALFI.

## Notes on positioning

- **Lead with DORA** — every fund admin has a DORA compliance gap. This is the door-opener, not "AI is exciting."
- **Show the Wirecard replay live** — it's the most concrete proof point in the pitch. Run it in the demo at `/api/v1/simulation/wirecard-replay`.
- **Cite the open-source repo** — institutional investors doing diligence will look at the code. Having it public and well-documented is a differentiator at pre-seed.
- **Never claim certifications you don't have** — the CSSF mapping is evidence of *readiness*, not *certification*. TIBER-EU is a test plan, not a completed audit. Say so.
