## What to do when one replies

1. Reply within 1 hour, max.
2. Offer 30-min Zoom in their timezone today or tomorrow.
3. Pre-prepare: 12-slide walk-through deck (build this beforehand).
4. Share the raw commit hash + OTS receipt + KPI sheet.
5. Be ready for hard questions: legal exposure, defamation, methodology critique.
6. Stay in the language: "operational-risk indicator", not "fraud". "Supervisory monitoring", not "collapse".
7. Don't pitch product or pricing on the first call. Pitch the story.
8. After the call, send a 1-page follow-up with all citations and verification paths.

## Hard questions to prep for

**Q: Is this defamation?**
A: No. The list is published as analytical opinion based exclusively on publicly available information. It does not allege fraud or wrongdoing by any named entity. The vindication criteria are broad and operational — supervisory action, audit qualification, leadership change, share-price stress — none of which are defamatory claims. The Article 10 ECHR protection for freedom of expression is the legal basis. Every claim about every entity is cited to public sources.

**Q: Are you trying to short these stocks?**
A: No, and the list does not predict share-price movements as a primary criterion. We have no position in any named entity. The Watch List is a public-interest analytical exercise.

**Q: Won't the entities sue you?**
A: They have a right of reply at daman.sharma.2310@gmail.com. We will publish any factual correction immediately. The list does not allege wrongdoing.

**Q: What if you're wrong about all 5?**
A: At the 18-month reveal date, if none of the 5 has triggered a vindication criterion, Genesis Swarm publicly retires this edition of the Watch List as unconfirmed. The cryptographic seal makes our forecast as falsifiable as a scientific hypothesis.

**Q: Aren't all these too big to fail?**
A: The list does not predict insolvency. It predicts operational-risk events: enforcement action, audit qualification, fine, leadership departure, share-price stress. UBS, Deutsche Bank, SocGen all face such events regularly. The Watch List is a specific, dated, falsifiable bet that the named 5 are statistically more exposed to such events in 2026-27 than the median of their peers.

**Q: Where can I verify the cryptographic commit?**
A: SHA-256 hash visible on the /watchlist page. Verification via OpenTimestamps at btc.calendar.opentimestamps.org/timestamp/{hash}. Methodology fully documented at /anchor.

**Q: Why now?**
A: I'm 16. I have an asymmetric risk profile: if I'm right, I'm the kid who called it. If I'm wrong, I retire one edition of a Watch List. I built the scoring engine from scratch; this is the cleanest public test of it.
